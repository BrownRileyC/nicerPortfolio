About Me

I was born and raised in the suburbs of Philadelphia. I attended William Penn Charter school and then studied psychology at the University of Vermont with a focus on developmental psychology. It's a fascinating field, in my opinion, and something very deserving of more study.

After graduating I decided to pursue a career working with young children, but not as a counselor and instead an educator. I was lucky to find a position working as a preschool and kindergarten prepratory teacher.

I loved working as a teacher and it was a privilege to see the children growing older and learning over the two years I spent at the Bright Horizons Child Development Center. While working there I discovered my interest in programming and development. I began to search for a way to enter the field, and I was fortunate enough to discover an intensive course of study that would help me to begin my career in web development.

While studying, I had many oportunities to learn valuable skills. One of the most important, I think, is to check the little things when I run into problems. Another vital skill is the ability both to work with others and to discuss my issues with others. I had many excellent classmates who helped me understand difficult concepts and who I helped in turn. Communication is a vital skill to have, and my interactions with my classmates showed me the power it can have to solve problems.
